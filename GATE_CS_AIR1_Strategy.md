# GATE CS Complete Strategy — Target: AIR 1 / IIT

## Core Philosophy

Concept-building comes from YouTube (syllabus-aligned, free, high quality). Heavy standalone textbooks are optional — use them only as reference when a doubt isn't clearing from video/notes. Your real weapon for AIR 1 is **relentless PYQ practice + revision cycles**, not the number of books you own.

## The One Book That Matters Most: PYQ Workbook

Pick **ONE** and stick to it — don't juggle multiple:

- **Made Easy – GATE CS Workbook** (most popular, well-sorted)
- **ACE Academy – GATE CS Combo** (alternative, equally solid)

This is non-negotiable for an AIR 1 target — it has short theory summaries + topic-sorted PYQs + practice sets in one place.

**Also use:** GateOverflow.in — free website with every PYQ discussed in depth, multiple solving approaches per question. AIR 1 aspirants live on this site during revision.

## AIR 1 Strategy — What Actually Separates Toppers

1. **Solve PYQs immediately after each topic** — don't wait till syllabus completion. This builds pattern recognition, which is 50% of GATE scoring.
2. **Maintain a formula + mistake notebook** — every silly mistake, tricky formula, and conceptual trap goes into one small notebook. Revise this notebook weekly.
3. **Timed mock tests from Day 1 of revision phase** — full-length GATE mocks (3 hrs) at least once a week in the last 3 months. Analyze every wrong answer — was it a concept gap or a silly mistake?
4. **Subject-wise weightage awareness** — spend time proportional to GATE marks distribution (Programming & DS, Algorithms, OS, DBMS, CN, TOC, COA carry the most weight historically; Compiler Design and Digital Logic carry less).
5. **Three revision cycles minimum:**
   - 1st pass: Learn concept + solve topic PYQs
   - 2nd pass: Revise notes + solve mixed PYQs (2–3 subjects together)
   - 3rd pass (final month): Only mock tests + error notebook + weak-topic drilling
6. **Track every mock score topic-wise**, not just overall — this tells you exactly where marks are being lost.
7. **Sleep, consistency, and daily small wins beat occasional 12-hour marathons.** GATE prep is a 6–10 month game, not a sprint.

## Bottom Line

You don't need a separate heavyweight book for every subject. YouTube channels + free notes build your concepts. One good PYQ workbook + GateOverflow builds your practice. What actually gets someone to AIR 1 is disciplined revision cycles, mock test analysis, and ruthless honesty about weak topics — not the size of your bookshelf.
